Is Osanix safe? Side effects to know

Two of the most-searched Osanix questions — answered plainly.

Osanix is sold as a food supplement for adults, not a medicine, and it contains no prescription drug. The ingredient most people notice is the caffeine — roughly a strong cup of coffee in the daily dose. If you are sensitive to stimulants, the effects usually reported are the ordinary caffeine ones: feeling wired, a faster heartbeat, or trouble sleeping when it is taken late. Taking it in the morning is the simple fix.

  • Contains caffeine — take it early, and count your other coffees
  • Cayenne and ginger can be uncomfortable on a genuinely empty or sensitive stomach
  • Not for under-18s, pregnancy or breastfeeding
  • On medication, or managing blood pressure, blood sugar or a heart condition? Ask a doctor or pharmacist first
  • Stick to the stated dose — doubling up does not double the effect
